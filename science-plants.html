Science – Plants Experiment Overview

The grade 2 science course will focus on “Plants” throughout the entire first term. Students will become familiar with the specific parts of a plant, examples of plants that we eat, what a plant needs in order to grow and change, as well as, the important role plants play in our environment.

Plants KWL (What I Know, What I Want to Know, What I Learned):

The sepal is what the students know about plants and the flower petals is what the students want to know. At the end of the term, we will add what the students have learned about plants.


Experiments

The grade 2 students will explore the growth of plants by performing hands on experiments. Stay posted for updates on how our plants are growing.


Lima Bean and Lentil Experiment

We will be exploring the process a plant takes to grow from a seed to a plant by comparing how lima beans and lentils grow. Each student is growing their own lima bean and lentil plant. Students are responsible for their seeds and will ensure that the seed is watered and has sun light.

Lima bean seeds in moist paper towel.


Lentil seed in moist paper towel.


As a class we are also investigating the importance of a plant’s four basic needs: water, sun light, soil, and nutrients. We have three separate cups that will test this.

Cup #1: The lima beans and lentils receive water and sun light.

Cup #2: The lima beans and lentils receive water, but no sun light.

Cup #3: The lima beans and lentils receive water, but no water.
