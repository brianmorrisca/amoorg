My experience at Chippewa taught me the importance of interactive learning

I learned a significant amount about teaching in my three week grade four teaching practicum with Chippewa Public School. I enjoyed experiencing the curriculum in the junior division and especially valued the strategies I learned. I was able to further develop my classroom management with the very active grade four class.

The most valuable technique I learned from this experience was the importance of interactive lessons and activities. Through the interactive, hands on multiplication lessons I prepared, students were able to understand the meaning of multiplication; not just memorize their multiplications table. The students were in power of their learning and were more enthusiastic. This experience was very rewarding as a new teacher.

Grade Four

My second teaching practicum was in a grade four classroom at Chippewa Public School. This teaching practicum was three weeks and I was responsible for teaching half the day.

I created an interactive mathematics unit for multiplication and assessed students in this unit. I created lessons in Balanced Literacy and conducted informal reading conferences with individual students during independent reading time. I taught a visual arts lesson, which integrated with their social studies unit on Medieval Times. All lessons and units were based on criteria from the Ontario curriculum.
